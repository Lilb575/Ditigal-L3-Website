Career Pathways

Here are some New Zealanders who rose above financial hardship through education, talent, and perseverance.

Janet Frame

Janet, born in 1924 in Dunedin, grew up in poverty and faced additional personal struggles, including a misdiagnosis of schizophrenia that led to years in psychiatric hospitals. Despite these difficulties, education provided her with a way forward. She pursued studies at the University of Otago and Dunedin Teachers' College, where she developed her talent for writing. Her literary success eventually allowed her to escape financial instability and build a sustainable career.

Jonah Lomu

Jonah Lomu, born in 1975 in Auckland, used his natural athletic talent to rise above financial hardship. Growing up in a working-class Tongan family, he experienced financial struggles, but his ability in rugby opened doors for him. At just 19 years old, he became the youngest player to debut for the All Blacks. His remarkable speed and strength made him a global rugby icon, securing financial stability and opportunities that lifted him and his family out of poverty.

Alexia Hilbertidou

Alexia Hilbertidou faced financial barriers and limited opportunities, but rather than letting this hold her back, she took advantage of education, leadership programs, and networking opportunities.
