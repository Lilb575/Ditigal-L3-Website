Janet Frame

Janet, born in 1924 in Dunedin, grew up in poverty and faced additional personal struggles, including a misdiagnosis of schizophernia that led to years in psychiatric hospitals. Despite these difficulties, education provided her with a way forward. She pursued studies at the University of Otago and Dunedin Teachers' College, where she developed her talent for writing. Her literary success eventually allowed her to escape financial instability and build a stustainable career.

Jonah Lomu

Jonah Lomu, born in 1975 in Auckland, used his natural athletic talent to rise above finanical hardship. Growing up in a working-class Tongan family, he experienced financial struggles, but his ability in rugby union opened doors for him. At just 19 years old, he became the youngest player to debut for the All blacks. His remarkable speed and strength made him a global rugby icon, securing financial stability and opportunities that lifted him and his family out of poverty.
